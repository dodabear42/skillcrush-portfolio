Hello, I'm a developer with a passion for inclusion.

A little bit about me.

I live in Michigan with my family of humans and animals. I've loved tech since I first talked my parents into allowing me to adopt an old used PC a friend was getting rid of. I was intrigued by PC components and how to make them work together. I eventually went on to earn a degree in Information Systems. The building websites aspect of tech is what stuck with me, and the sense of accomplishment I felt when I got the puzzle pieces of a site to fit together the way I wanted it to was motivating.

As I continue to learn and grow as a developer, I build on my ability to create websites that are accessible to all. I want my sites to be created with people in mind who have various abilities because some barriers should not be there in the first place.

HTML

I care about creating files that are easy-to-read and update, and I use the semantic power of HTML5.

CSS

Styles change sometimes. I build CSS in a predictable manner, so if someone needs to come back to it later, it'll be easy to navigate and change.
